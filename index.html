RCB Players Profile

Virat Kholi

Virat Kohli is a top-order Indian cricketer, widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen of all time. He was born in Delhi on November 5, 1988. Kohli plays for Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) in the IPL and previously captained the Indian national team in all three formats. He holds numerous batting records, including the most centuries in ODI cricket and the most runs in IPL history. He also led India to victory in the 2011 Cricket World Cup.

phil Salt

Phil Salt is an English cricketer who played for the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) in the IPL 2025 season, where he was a key opening batsman. He was signed for ₹11.5 crore and scored 403 runs in 13 matches at a strike rate of 175.98, including four half-centuries.

Rajat patidar

Rajat Patidar is an Indian cricketer who plays for the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) in the IPL. He is a right-handed batsman known for his aggressive strokeplay. In his first season as captain, he led RCB to their maiden IPL title in 2025, fulfilling the long-standing dream of the franchise and its fans.

devdutt Padikkal

Devdutt Padikkal, a batsman for Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB), recently rejoined the team after playing for Rajasthan Royals and Lucknow Super Giants. He was bought back by RCB for INR 2 crore in IPL 2025. In the current season, he scored 247 runs in 10 matches with a strike rate of 150.60, including two half-centuries, before being ruled out due to a hamstring injury. Mayank Agarwal replaced him for the remainder of the season

Jitesh Sharma

Jitesh Sharma is an Indian cricketer who plays for Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) in the Indian Premier League (IPL). He is a wicket-keeper and right-handed batsman known for his finishing abilities and handy cameos. In the 2025 IPL season, Jitesh joined RCB and played a crucial role in their campaign. He also earned his T20I debut for India after a strong IPL showing in 2023 with Punjab Kings.

krunal Pandya

Krunal Pandya, a key player for Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB), recently played a crucial role in their IPL 2025 victory. He was named Player of the Match in the final for his match-winning spell of 2/17 against Punjab Kings, helping RCB secure their first IPL title. Pandya's performance, including a key 2/17 spell, was instrumental in RCB's six-run win. He also celebrated the victory with a viral "Khaby Lame" pose, a cheeky response to critics who questioned his move to RCB.

jacob Bethell

Jacob Bethell is a young English cricketer who played for Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) during the 2025 IPL season. He's a left-handed batter and slow left-arm orthodox bowler, known for his explosive hitting and valuable contributions to the team. During his time with RCB, he notably partnered with Virat Kohli, forming a strong opening duo and gaining insights from the legendary batsman.

Liam Livingstone

Liam Livingstone is an English cricketer who plays for the Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) in the Indian Premier League (IPL). He is a right-handed batsman and right-arm off-spin bowler. In the 2025 IPL season, RCB acquired Livingstone for a price of ₹8.75 crore, according to The Economic Times.

Suyash Sharma

Suyash Sharma is an Indian cricketer, primarily a right-arm leg-spinner, who plays for Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) in the Indian Premier League. He was born on May 15, 2003. Hailing from Delhi, he began his cricketing journey in local club cricket before making his mark at the junior level. He was acquired by RCB ahead of the 2025 IPL season.

josh Hazlewood

Josh Hazlewood, the Australian fast bowler, plays for Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) in the IPL. He was picked up by RCB for INR 7.75 crore at the IPL 2022 Mega Auction. Known for his accuracy and sharp bouncers, Hazlewood played a crucial role in RCB's IPL 2025 campaign, where he also won the Purple Cap. He returned to RCB for the IPL 2025 playoffs after missing some matches due to a shoulder niggle.

Bhuvneshwar Kumar

Bhuvneshwar Kumar, the Indian pacer, is now part of the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) team in the Indian Premier League (IPL). Known for his swing bowling, he's a valuable addition to the RCB bowling attack. He was acquired by RCB in the 2025 auction for a significant price. Bhuvneshwar has also won the IPL's Purple Cap twice in the past.
